Revitalizing "Think Green at Work"

Analyzing an FMCG company's internal sustainability campaign & proposing a path to success.

Industry

FMCG

Campaign

"Think Green at Work"

Problem

Low adoption, undermining ESG. Not seen as urgent.

Challenge

Measure behavioral change, not just awareness.

Campaign Effectiveness: AIDA Analysis

Evaluating "Think Green at Work" using the Attention–Interest–Desire–Action model.

Attention

Achieved: Colorful posters and emails likely grabbed initial attention.

However, passive methods yield short-lived impact.

Interest

Low: Seen as "nice" but not "urgent or relevant." No link to daily work.

Lacks personal relevance.

Desire

Very Low: Without clear benefit (WIIFM?), desire to participate is minimal.

No incentives or connection to values.

Action

Minimal: Low recycling and participation. Result of no desire/interest.

Ultimate goal not met.

Conclusion: The campaign gained Attention but failed to generate Interest and Desire, leading to poor Action.

Employee Engagement Measurement Plan

A multi-faceted approach to track awareness, sentiment, and behavioral change.

Surveys

Baseline & Pulse Surveys:

  • Gauge awareness, attitudes, and barriers.
  • Track changes post-relaunch.
  • Collect feedback and suggestions.

Digital Tracking

Observable Metrics:

  • Recycling Rates: Measure volume of recycled materials.
  • Resource Consumption: Track energy, water, paper usage.
  • Intranet Engagement: Monitor content views, forum activity.

Ambassador Feedback

Insights from "Green Champions":

  • Establish a network of enthusiastic ambassadors.
  • Understand ground-level sentiment.
  • Identify local successes and challenges.

Identifying What Needs to Be Fixed

Diagnosing core issues with the original "Think Green at Work" campaign.

Tone

Problem: "Nice" but not "urgent." Lacks impact and connection.

Fix: Infuse urgency, highlight impact. Be empowering and inspiring.

Format

Problem: Static posters and emails are easily ignored.

Fix: Use interactive workshops, gamification, digital platforms.

Incentive

Problem: No clear "What's In It For Me?" (WIIFM). No recognition.

Fix: Implement recognition system, team rewards. Link to company benefits.

Alignment

Problem: Not relevant to daily work or company goals. Seems disconnected.

Fix: Link to ESG commitments and business objectives. Show individual contribution. Leadership support is key.

Relaunch Strategy: Tactics & KPIs

Actionable recommendations for a successful campaign relaunch.

New Engagement Tactics

  • Leadership Kick-off: Emphasize strategic importance.
  • Gamified "Green Challenge": Make participation fun and competitive.
  • Interactive Workshops: Focus on practical skills and idea sharing.
  • Visible Progress Dashboards: Show real-time impact.
  • Storytelling & Recognition: Share successes, appreciate participants.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Participation Rate: +30% increase in active participation.
  • Recycling Volume: +20% increase in sorted materials.
  • Waste Reduction: -10% decrease in office waste.
  • Sustainability Survey Scores: +10 point increase in surveys.
  • Number of Green Ideas: Increase in new suggestions.
